01

Overview

Metabolomics is the profiling of small molecules (metabolites) in complex biological matrices. The Frezza Lab is equipped with Thermo Scientific Orbitrap Exploris 120 and 240 mass spectrometers, coupled to a Vanquish Horizon UHPLC system. We focus on primary, water-soluble metabolites essential for growth, development, and reproduction, using a semi-targeted approach with an in-house metabolite library.

Note: Our HILIC-based platform is optimised for polar metabolites — nucleotides, amino acids, organic acids, and sugar phosphates. Reversed-phase methods are not in scope.

02

Experimental Design

A well-designed experiment is crucial for success. After an initial collaboration inquiry, a meeting is scheduled with Prof. Christian Frezza and the team to discuss project background, aims, and experimental strategies. Please provide a brief project summary (in PowerPoint or Word format) along with any preliminary data.

04

Data Acquisition

Data acquisition is performed using HILIC coupled with high-resolution MS. Pooled QC samples are run at regular intervals (every 6–8 injections) to monitor instrument performance. Both full-scan MS and MS2 fragmentation methods are used, depending on experimental needs.

05

Data Processing

Data processing includes normalization of raw signal intensities and natural abundance correction (for tracer-labelled studies). Data visualization is achieved through PCA plots, heat maps, bar graphs, and volcano plots.

06

Biological Interpretation & Publication

The biological interpretation of metabolomic data is an area we are passionate about and highly value. We will discuss the results extensively with collaborators, together with other lines of complementary data, until a conclusion can be reached for the next step of the project.

If a follow-up study is required, the process is repeated until the results are ready for publication. Where required, our in-house bioinformatician can provide further assistance on the integrative analysis of multi-omics datasets, and with final data representation in figures.

Data Deposition: For accepted manuscripts, we can deposit raw metabolomic data files on platforms such as MetaboLights and Metabolomics Workbench as requested by journals.

07

Experimental Design Considerations

Considerations include selecting appropriate biological, technical, and analytical replicates. For cell culture studies, at least five technical replicates per condition are recommended, while animal studies typically require at least four biological replicates.

08

Stable Isotope Tracing

Stable isotope tracers (e.g., ¹³C, ¹⁵N, ²H) are used to monitor metabolic fluxes. To ensure accuracy, experiments must minimize metabolic perturbation and perform corrections for natural isotope abundance.

09

Sample Replication

Replication is essential for robust data. Biological replicates are sourced independently, while technical replicates (repeating the entire procedure) help assess variance in sample processing.

10

Sample Preparation Considerations

Special considerations include consistent handling of cultured cells, including rapid quenching and uniform extraction. For media, tissues, and biofluids, careful processing and pooling of samples is critical to ensure data quality.

11

Quantitation

Quantitative data are provided as relative signal intensities. For absolute quantitation, an internal standard dilution approach is applied. Normalization varies for tissues, biofluids, and cell samples.

12

LC-MS Limitations

Although LC-MS detects hundreds to thousands of metabolites with high sensitivity, it is not highly precise due to variations in ionization efficiency and potential isobaric interference. For key findings, further validation may be necessary.

13

Data Analysis Overview

Our analysis pipeline includes:

  • Primary data reporting with peak area and metabolite metadata.
  • PCA plots for assessing sample similarities and detecting outliers.
  • Heat maps for visualizing metabolite patterns.
  • Bar graphs and volcano plots to highlight significant changes.
  • Stacked bar graphs for tracer-labelled data.

14

Protocols & Materials

Materials: LC-MS grade Methanol, Acetonitrile, ultrapure water, and standards (e.g., Valine-d8). Additional supplies include Eppendorf tubes, cell scrapers, autosampler vials, cryo-labels, and dry ice.

A. Cultured Cells

  1. Count cells and calculate extraction buffer (500 µl per 10⁶ cells).
  2. Remove medium, wash with PBS, and aspirate thoroughly.
  3. Place plate on dry ice; add extraction buffer and incubate on a dry ice–methanol bath for 20 minutes.
  4. Scrape cells, transfer to pre-chilled tubes, shake in a Thermomixer at 4 °C for 15 minutes, and centrifuge.
  5. Collect the top 80 % of the supernatant and prepare a pooled QC sample from equal aliquots.

B. Cell Culture Media (CCM)

  1. Centrifuge 80 µl of CCM to remove cells.
  2. Add the supernatant to 350 µl of extraction solution on dry ice.
  3. Mix, shake at 4 °C for 15 minutes, centrifuge, and collect the top 80 % of the supernatant.
  4. Pool equal volumes for a QC sample.

C. Tissues

  1. Weigh and label tissue (10–20 mg recommended).
  2. Add extraction solution at 25 µl per mg tissue.
  3. Homogenize using a Precellys tissue homogenizer.
  4. Shake, centrifuge, and carefully collect the supernatant.
  5. Prepare a pooled sample.

D. Plasma / Serum

  1. Collect blood using a heparin tube (or allow serum to clot).
  2. Centrifuge promptly to separate plasma/serum, avoiding the buffy coat.
  3. Mix 40 µl of plasma with 360 µl of extraction solution on dry ice.
  4. Vortex, shake at 4 °C, centrifuge, and filter using an Amicon Ultra-0.5 centrifugal filter unit.

E. Genomic NA Digestion

  1. Isolate genomic DNA using an appropriate kit (e.g., QIAGEN DNeasy Blood & Tissue Kit).
  2. Digest using Nucleoside Digestion Mix (NEB) as per manufacturer instructions.
  3. Add water and methanol, vortex, centrifuge, and collect the supernatant.
